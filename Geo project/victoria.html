Victoria!

Geography

Victoria city is located on the southern end of Vancouver island and is surrounded by the Northern Pacific Ocean. It consists of mainly rolling lowlands scattered with granite rocks that goes up to a height of 300 meters. On the North and West shoreline, cliffs, lagoons, and spits are located there formed by past glacial action. There are lots of municipal parks as well.

Climate

Victoria city experiences moderate temperatures all year round and also experiences some of the most mild winters in Canada. Victoria city's daily temperatures rarely fall below zero degrees Celsius and rarely go above 30 degrees Celsius. The city also experiences lots of sunshine with rarely any snow. Victoria city also has one of the lowest annual rainfall in the Province. During winters is when it is rainy and experiences very dry summers.

Facts About Victoria

- Victoria city experiences only an average of around 600 millimeters of rain per year.
- The population is around 344,615 people.
- The highest ever recorded temperature in Victoria city was 35.3 degrees Celsius.
- The lowest ever recorded temperature in Victoria city was -15.6 degrees Celsius.
