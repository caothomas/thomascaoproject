Wildlife!

The Pacific Maritime has a very big and diverse collection of wildlife with over 200 unique species that are found there. The Pacific Maritime ecozone extends all the way to the islands of British Columbia and passes through part of the ring of fire as well. This means that many of the wildlife are species of fish and and other aquatic mammals. Fish and other wildlife can not only be found in the ocean but in fjords and inlets too. Here are some of the unique wildlife that can be found in the Pacific Maritime:

Snowshoe Hare
Mountain Lion
Bald Eagle
Great Blue Heron
Blue-winged Teal
Lewis Woodpecker
Cedar Waxwing
Harbour Seal
Blue Whale
Pacific Giant Salamander
Leatherback Turtle
Mountain Goat
